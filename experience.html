Disney Research Pittsburgh

I am currently an intern at Disney Research Pittsburgh. I work as an artist primarily creating 3d computer models for various projects. Working at Disney has allowed me to significantly improve my modeling and texturing skills in both Maya and Zbrush. I have also benefitted through an increased understanding of both digital fabrication and animation.

Jim Henson’s Creature Shop

In the summer of 2011 I was fortunate enough to work as an intern At Jim Henson’s Creature Shop in New York City. During my time there I got to do a variety of tasks from building puppet rods and running props and characters to and from set, to design and building a few props, costumes and minor characters for various productions.

ScareHouse

During the fall of 2010, I was hired as a makeup artist and actor at Pittsburgh’s Scarehouse. While working I was able to improve my makeup skills as well as gain experience working in a fast paced production environment.

Red Cup Pong

From 2009-2010, I was lead designer for Red Cup Pong. I worked primarily in Photoshop creating many designs for merchandise as well as the brands logo. During my time I received constant and particular feedback on my work, and had to adapt my designs quickly to fit the needs of my employer.

Theater

During my four years at Carnegie Mellon I’ve helped on several productions, With the university club theater Scotch’n’Soda, I worked on Rocky Horror Picture Show, and Reefer Madness as a makeup artist and prop maker. I was also head of makeup artist for a production of Eurydice. For the university’s school of drama I was commissioned to create complex blood effects for a several scenes in A Maid’s Tragedy.
